Concept of home

ecobee
ecobee is a smart home technology company in Canada, founded in 2007. It started as a smart thermostat company with room sensors, and expanded to offering a full suite of home security products including smart light switches, indoor cameras, door and window sensor.
My Contributions
I worked at ecobee for 4 years between 2016 and 2020. I led software design for ecobee's residential products and services to ensure a streamlined and consistent customer experience. My role later mainly focused on facilitating cross-team discussions on product strategy and UX alignment that affects multiple squads.
01.
What is the concept of home?
ecobee has come a very long way.
ecobee began as a smart thermostat company and maintained its course for 10 years. Then came a Switch+, a voice controlled smart light switch. As smart home devices became more mainstream for the general public, ecobee started exploring what other products would be useful in creating a "whole home comfort" — a home where everything works seamlessly, giving you a peace of mind.

With the business evolving in this direction, the mobile app also had to adapt to enhance the user experience. Our goal was to elevate the mobile app experience from being thermostat-centric for a single device home to becoming home-centric for a multi-device home.
02.
Scope of work
TLDR: basically re-built the software solution
ecobee built amazing hardware products, and there was a huge gap between the quality when it came to software products. ecobee wanted to establish its place as a leading smart home technology company, and the product team had a very clear vision and roadmap.

First, we needed to re-structure information architecture for both UX and backend. Existing system architecture tied everything to a thermostat, and it was crucial to create a new system architecture to support the future of ecobee home ecosystem.

Next was research to understand the behaviour of our future customers. How do they control devices? When do they control devices? What are the pain points and opportunities?

Lastly, apply the insights gained from research to enhance the user experience and interface of the mobile app. Easy right? So 3 giant projects packaged in one.
03.
Research, research, research!
Thankfully ecobee had a dedicated research team to support this.
Research Method:
Customer interviews & rapid prototype testing with different customer segments
• Single device (thermostat) owners x 5
• Multiple device (Thermostat + Light switch) owners x 5
• Non-ecobee smart home device owners x 5

Key findings:
• Multi-device owners want to control the overall state of their Home, not just room by room
• Most convenient method of control at the time will be used (Motion > Voice > Manual)
• Expect app to surface relevant information and simplify steps to control and manage
• More information the better — it's not about control, it's about having a peace of mind
• Device-specific UI makes it difficult to understand or control their Home
• Users want to access frequently-used features on app launch
04.
Get the whole team involved!
Free snacks always work.
Working closely with the technical director, entire mobile engineering team, we wanted to make sure everyone staffed on this project fully understood why we are making this major change, and be 100% on board to make it work.

This is where design workshops become handy — you can easily encourage and guide non-designers to think like designers by isolating a problem space, providing context, and involving them in a quick sketching exercise like Crazy Eights.

For the mobile team I ran two workshops, one for navigation and the other one for home overview screen. This is where design needed the most support, and it's always helpful to have engineers on your side when you present to the C-suite.

Sometimes it's best to create a blue-sky concept that works and work backwards to figure out a feasible roll-out plan.

05.
Actual design that was built
~ the concept of home ~
The app's content is organized to align with the mental model that places 'Home' at the highest level of the hierarchy. Setting up a home became a part of new user onboarding, and existing users are asked to set up a home at app launch. This change laid the foundation framework for the business's future vision.

Mobile app redesign included new navigation patterns, and a home overview screen with a new card UI system to support all device combination. We created consistent UX for both single and multiple device homes, future-proofing and leaving space for more device types and home service. The entire mobile app went through an overhaul to adapt native mobile guidelines while supporting our unique device specific controls. This change would positively impact the overall perception of our app, and ultimately our brand.

Of course we tested all of these — 5 ecobee & 5 non-ecobee users successfully identified and located features using the new navigation structure, and were able to perform device-specific tasks easily. Task completion rate was 100% with a score of 8.5 out of 10 on ease of use.

06.
Result
ecobee whole home comfort.
This fundamental change of our app changing from a Smart Thermostat app to a Home-based app allowed ecobee to launch the next series of innovative hardware product with no blockers on software or system architecture changes. ecobee went on to develop a security system using the Thermostat as the security hub, and launched smart devices like an indoor camera, door and window contact sensor, using room sensor as an occupancy sensor. The new design allowed us to seamlessly introduce the new security overview for the home.
