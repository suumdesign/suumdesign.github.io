Co.Lab branding & website

Co.Lab
Co.Lab offers product management training programs for tech professionals who wants to get into the product management track. Their classes are unique as they don't rely on the traditional online teaching method, and instead give you hands-on, real-world tech experience.
My Contributions
I helped develop their brand identity and created their website when they first launched. Over time, as the product evolved into an open online education platform, and then back to offering product management courses.
01.
Branding & website requirements
It's time for an upgrade.
Just like any start-up, Co.Lab continuously launched their product and pivoted as needed. For this year, they wanted to focus heavily on product management education track. They wanted to revamp their branding and website to appeal to a wider range of audience, both new and experienced tech professionals.

Co.Lab's goal was to stand out from its competitors, keep the friendly characteristics of the branding while elevating the overall mood and tone of the website to attract more customers. We wanted to have a very clear, concise message about what Co.Lab does differently and about the two program tracks offered. Users needed enough information about both programs to be able to choose which one to apply for.
02.
New year, new me
Exploring brand personalities.
Previous logo played with the idea of shapes and we wanted to continue using that theme, as shapes are a good metaphorical representation of building blocks that makes a product. After reviewing options, we chose an overlapping shape that looks like a Venn diagram which is often used by product managers. We wanted to reflect the personality of the brand we defined earlier in the process.

Co.Lab is knowledgeable & reliable — we are always there for you to solve problems together and support you through your career path. Co.Lab is collaborative — we will connect you with the right people, right companies and right mentors. Co.Lab will keep you accountable — We will guide you but not do the work for you. We'll push you to be better.
03.
Process
Branding needs customer feedback too.
Based on the final logo, I drafted 3 different landing page designs that are drastically different, each to convey different emotion.

We showed 3 design direction options to 16 students, and asked them for first impression. They also had to rate each one for the brand personality, example: Which option is most "trustworthy" to you? Based on the research result and Co.Lab co-founders input, we decided on a direction.

User can easily compare the two programs and make an informed choice.

04.
Website redesign
What else changed?
As per client's request, we did not create a full branding book package. Co.Lab needed a quick way to update their website to launch their new courses in the fall. We implemented the new brand in the website by introducing the new colour palette, serif fonts and sharp edge UI elements.

In terms of UX, we created backlinks for two programs, and dedicated sections on comparing the two programs to help users choose. We moved away from lengthy copy and long scroll to a more concise information and used images to convey our messaging.

Next steps:
Currently Co.Lab uses an external form to consolidate and manage their students and there is a limitation on customization in terms of both user experience and interface. Next phase of the project would include a fully native onboarding process for students.
