Real estate investing platform

About Lotly
Lotly is a Toronto-based prop-tech company that specializes in shared equity of residential homes.
My Contributions
As a member of the founding team, I worked closely with the CEO and the co-founder to build the MVP for the online investing platform.
01.
Research market fit
Who will invest in our product?
Shared equity program is a relatively new concept in Canada, which meant we didn't know who our customer segment was. They could be anyone between traditional real estate investors, early adopters who are interested in prop-tech, stock & crypto investors, alternative asset hunters (wine, farmland, whiskey, etc).

We tested our hypothesis about the target user by surveying people who have invested in any type of asset before, and their journey. Why did they pick that asset type? What triggered their first ever purchase of the asset? How do they feel about it looking back?
02.
Testing the waters, building our MVP
Test, test, test.
The Lotly co-founders come from a product background, which meant they fully understood and were on board with rapid testing with real customers. Our first landing page was a simple one-pager asking users to sign up to be on the waitlist. We benchmarked what Robinhood did pre-launch, because what better way to learn other than actually getting real customers?

Design goal was very clear — explain the concept of shared equity, benefits of our fund, prompt investors to sign up and tell us how much they intend to invest. There were many new ideas being presented to the user, and we opted for friendly yet educational tone and messaging. Working closely with our data team, we created multiple interactive tools to show users projected return.

Our MVP journey:
• User tested live prototype for comprehension
• Collected user info on the waitlist
• Iterate content placement based on learnings from Hotjar
• Organically collected 1000+ user emails

Based on the learnings from our waitlist program, we started building the Lotly online platform.

Before launching our product, we implemented a very simple waitlist system to gauge demand.

03.
Optimize onboarding
Anyone else know what KYC is?
Before working at Lotly, I have never heard of these words: exempt securities, KYC, Subscription form, Offering memorandum, etc. TLDR, to make a private real estate investment, there are a million requirements and documents needed.

UX practices I used:
• Ask for low-risk, easy information first
• Organize and group information
• Give user a sense of progress, a visual cue
• Confirm and reward user after completing a step
• Clearly communicate why we need certain information
• Offer in-person support where applicable

Process:
• Multiple meetings with our partners (law firm & portfolio manager)
• Defined requirements and which part can be legally digitized
• Parsed the original PDF forms and created an online application
• Each step was built in a modular format to be easily rearranged
• Compared payment processing options and integrated with Zumrail

Solution:
There are plenty of online resources on best UX practices for designing long forms. Using these principles, we broke the onboarding process into meaningful steps. While we moved information collection and payments online for efficiency, we kept ID verification handled by a real person to maintain trust and security for users.

After many meetings with our law firm & portfolio manager, we transformed a 10 page legal jargon to a user-friendly form.

04.
Investor experience
Onboarding and beyond!
Once our product was ready to launch, all our waitlist customers received communication and a direct CTA to invest in the Lotly fund. Using real-customers as a testing ground, we went through many iterations based on the drop-rate of each step of the funnel.

Process:
• Each step was built in a modular format to be easily rearranged
• After each test documented end-to-end funnel drop off rate every week
• Using Hotjar, monitored user interaction with each section
• Talked to customers who signed up and didn't complete transaction

Solution:
After trying multiple iterations of user onboarding, we built an investor dashboard where user can clearly see each step and get a sense of completion. Additional modules were added to build more trust including a list of other investments, downloadable documents and a link to book a call with our team.

Beyond the digital experience, our account manager personally reached out via call and text after a user signed up. As a new company, these direct interactions helped build trust and put users at ease. Once onboarding was complete, users could access a dashboard summarizing their investment, designed to feel familiar to those using other financial platforms.

Weekly funnel review with page view & drop-rate to identify improvement areas.

05.
Monitor and iterate
Lotly fund is live!
After many mini iterations our site is live, and works like a well-oiled machine. We have successfully raised over $400K in retail fund. The product is continuously evolving as we engage with our customers on a daily basis. We have added more interactive tools and FAQs to reduce the necessity for in-person support. I also collaborate closely with the growth lead to incorporate SEO keywords, manage social media ads, and consistently seek ways to enhance our product.

As of 2025, The Lotly GTA Fund is now closed. Through our mission to make homeownership more accessible, we saw a greater need to help homeowners improve their financial health. We're now focused on financing homeowners while continuing to support existing investors.
